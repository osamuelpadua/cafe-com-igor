# Landing Page Patterns Reference

## Standard Section Sequence (Sales / VSL Page)

This sequence is battle-tested for conversion. Sections can be reordered or removed based on content, but the core flow (Hook → Problem → Solution → Proof → Offer → CTA) should be preserved.

---

## 1. TOPBAR
**Purpose:** Urgency, social proof, or single benefit statement  
**Layout:** Full-width colored bar, centered text, small font  
**Content:** "🔥 X people joined · Free for 7 days · Limited spots"  
**Notes:** Optional but effective. Keep under 60 characters.

---

## 2. HERO
**Purpose:** Capture attention, state the main promise  
**Layout:** Full-width, large headline, subheadline, optional video, primary CTA  
**Content hierarchy:**
1. Eyebrow label (optional) — small, uppercase, colored
2. H1 — biggest text on page, 1–2 lines max
3. Subheadline — clarifies the promise, 1–3 lines
4. Social proof micro-element (optional) — "★★★★★ 4.8 from 2.3k reviews"
5. Primary CTA button — specific action ("Get Instant Access" beats "Buy Now")
6. Hero media — video embed, product image, or illustration

**Common mistakes:**
- H1 too vague ("Transform Your Life") — be specific
- CTA below the fold on mobile
- No supporting text below the button (risk reducers help: "No credit card · Cancel anytime")

---

## 3. PAIN / PROBLEM BLOCK
**Purpose:** Mirror the reader's current reality so they feel understood  
**Layout:** Centered text, optionally with icon+text list of pain points  
**Content patterns:**
- Opening question: "You already tried X, and it didn't work. Here's why."
- 3–5 bullet pain points with ❌ or similar markers
- Emotional close: "This isn't your fault. It's the pattern."

**Notes:**
- Do NOT rush to the solution here. Sit in the pain longer than feels comfortable.
- Avoid blaming the reader — frame pain as systemic, not personal failure.

---

## 4. DIAGNOSIS / INSIGHT BLOCK
**Purpose:** Name the real root cause (creates "aha" moment)  
**Layout:** Often a standout box, blockquote, or contrasting background section  
**Content:** 
- Reframe the problem ("It's not X. It's Y.")
- Introduce the mechanism ("The real reason this keeps happening is...")
- Bridge to the solution without revealing it yet

**Notes:** This is often the most persuasive section. Don't skip it.

---

## 5. AGITATION
**Purpose:** Intensify the problem before offering relief  
**Layout:** Short paragraphs, rhetorical questions, or "what happens if you don't" framing  
**Content:** Show the cost of inaction — time, money, emotional pain  
**Notes:** Keep this section brief (2–4 paragraphs). Long agitation feels manipulative.

---

## 6. SOLUTION REVEAL / VIRADA
**Purpose:** Introduce the product as the answer to the pain diagnosed above  
**Layout:** Authority statement → product name → brief description  
**Content:**
- Authority bridge ("After X years of Y, I found...")
- Product introduction (name, bold, centered)
- One-sentence positioning statement
- What makes this different from everything they've tried

---

## 7. PRODUCT PRESENTATION
**Purpose:** Show the product, establish credibility  
**Layout:** Product image/cover + name + subtitle, centered  
**Notes:** For digital products, show a 3D book mockup or device frame. Physical presence matters even for digital goods.

---

## 8. BENEFITS / WHAT YOU'LL LEARN
**Purpose:** Translate features into outcomes the reader wants  
**Layout:** Icon + headline + description rows, or grouped by category  
**Content patterns:**
- Group by outcome category (not by module/chapter)
- Lead each benefit with the transformation, not the technique
- ✅ "Understand why you keep attracting the wrong person — and break the cycle for good"
- ❌ "Module 3: Pattern Recognition"

**Notes:**
- 5–12 benefits is optimal. More feels overwhelming; fewer feels thin.
- For long benefit lists, group under 3–4 category headers with icons.

---

## 9. SOCIAL PROOF / TESTIMONIALS
**Purpose:** Reduce skepticism with real transformations  
**Layout options:**
- Grid of 3 cards (name, photo optional, quote, result)
- Screenshot strip (for online products with chat/comment proof)
- Single large featured quote with name and context

**Each testimonial should contain:**
1. Situation before ("I had tried everything...")
2. Specific transformation ("After applying step 4...")
3. Concrete result ("Now I can finally...")
4. Name + identifying detail (city, profession, or age)

**Notes:**
- Screenshots feel more authentic than designed cards for digital products
- Include at least one testimonial from someone skeptical before buying

---

## 10. AUTHORITY / WHO IS THIS PERSON
**Purpose:** Establish credibility of the creator  
**Layout:** Photo left, bio right (or centered column)  
**Content:**
- Personal story (briefly) — especially the "I was in your shoes" moment
- Credentials that matter to THIS audience (not general resume)
- Social proof numbers (followers, students, results)

**Notes:**
- Lead with the transformation the creator went through, not titles
- Keep it short — this is NOT the main event, it supports the offer

---

## 11. OFFER / PRICING
**Purpose:** Make the value proposition concrete and the decision easy  
**Layout:** Centered, price prominent, strikethrough original price if discounted  
**Components:**
- What's included (brief list)
- Original price (crossed out)
- Current price (large, bold)
- CTA button
- Payment options / security badges
- Risk reducer text below button ("30-day money-back guarantee")

**Notes:**
- Always anchor with original price before showing discount
- Show total value (what all pieces would cost separately) for bundles
- Payment badge icons reduce friction significantly

---

## 12. GUARANTEE
**Purpose:** Remove the last barrier to purchase (risk reversal)  
**Layout:** Seal/badge graphic + headline + body text  
**Content:** Be specific: "7 days, 100% refund, no questions asked, one click"  
**Notes:** Position BEFORE the final CTA, not after. People need reassurance at decision time.

---

## 13. FINAL CTA / DECISION BLOCK
**Purpose:** Last emotional push + simplest possible path to buy  
**Layout:** Two-column or centered — "Option A (status quo)" vs "Option B (transformation)"  
**Content:**
- Restate the cost of inaction briefly
- Restate the promise of the product
- Single, clear CTA button
- Price reminder

---

## 14. FAQ
**Purpose:** Handle objections without a salesperson  
**Layout:** Accordion (click to expand) or simple Q&A list  
**Content:** Answer the real objections:
- "Is this for me?" (specificity objection)
- "Does it really work?" (skepticism objection)
- "What format is it?" (logistics objection)
- "What if I don't like it?" (risk objection)
- "How long until I see results?" (urgency/patience objection)

**Notes:** 4–6 FAQs is standard. More suggests too many unresolved objections.

---

## 15. FOOTER
**Layout:** Logo, copyright, legal links (Privacy Policy, Terms of Use, Refund Policy)  
**Notes:** Keep minimal. Legal links are required. Avoid navigation that leads people away.

---

## Visual Hierarchy Rules for Landing Pages

### Above the fold (critical)
- H1 must be readable at a glance
- CTA button must be visible without scrolling (desktop)
- Value proposition must be understood in under 5 seconds

### Color usage
- 1 primary CTA color (warm: orange, gold, green — never blue for CTAs on dark BGs)
- Alternating section backgrounds for rhythm (dark → light → dark → light)
- Pain sections: slightly darker or cooler tone
- Solution/offer sections: warmer, brighter tone

### Typography scale (suggested)
- H1: 40–56px, bold, tight line-height
- H2 (section titles): 28–36px, bold
- H3 (subheads): 20–24px, semibold
- Body: 16–18px, regular, 1.6 line-height
- Captions / labels: 12–14px, uppercase, spaced

### Button anatomy
- Min height: 52px
- Padding: 16px 40px
- Font: bold, 16–18px
- Text: action verb + outcome ("Get Instant Access", "Start My Transformation")
- Secondary text below: risk reducer in small font

### Section spacing
- Between sections: 80–120px
- Inside section (content gap): 24–40px
- Max content width: 760–860px centered

---

## Conversion Anti-Patterns to Avoid

❌ No CTA in the first screenful  
❌ Generic CTAs ("Click Here", "Submit", "Buy")  
❌ Showing price before establishing value  
❌ Guarantee buried below the fold or in footer  
❌ Too many navigation links (let people leave)  
❌ Testimonials without specificity (just "Great product!")  
❌ Benefits written as features ("14 chapters") not outcomes  
❌ Hero headline that could apply to any product in any category
